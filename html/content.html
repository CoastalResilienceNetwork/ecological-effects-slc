Cumulative risk scores represent a pools percent risk out of the total possible risk calculated by: ([Risk1] + [Risk2] + [Risk3] + [Risk4]/160)*100) This is the sum of the risk scores for each individual risk variable divided by the highest possible risk score, in this case 160 (4 variables with 40 as the highest value). "low" represents pools with scores <20%, "medium" >=20 - 60%, and "high" > 60%. For more information on how each individual risk and solution variable was calculated, visit the Coastal Resilience Apps page.

Choose Type of Flooding

Frequent flooding is expected to occur daily to every other day. Semi-frequent flooding is expected to occur on a weekly to monthly basis. Infrequent flooding might occur one to several times a year. Rare flooding are extreme events that occur once in multiple decades. 2016 US Army Corps LiDAR data were used to create the flood elevation extents. Horizontal Positional Accuracy is compiled to meet 1 m at 95% confidence level and Vertical Positional Accuracy of 19.6 cm at a 95% confidence level (10 cm RMSE).
